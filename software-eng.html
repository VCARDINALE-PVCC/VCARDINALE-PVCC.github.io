Ch 7: Software Engineering

Term Definition
Software engineering The process of designing, developing, testing, and maintaining software.
Integrated Development Environment (IDE) A software application that provides comprehensive facilities for software development in one central interface.
Association for Computing Machinery (ACM)* A professional organization for computing professionals that promotes the development and advancement of computing as a science and a profession.
Purpose of ACM To advance computing as a science and profession, to support the professional growth of its members, and to promote policies and research that benefit society.
Benefits of ACM memberships Access to publications, career resources, networking opportunities, and discounted conference registrations.
Student ACM membership A discounted membership for students that includes access to research and career resources.
Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers (IEEE)** A professional association for electronic and electrical engineering and computer science professionals.
Purpose of IEEE To advance technology for the benefit of humanity.
Benefits of IEEE membership Access to publications, career resources, networking opportunities, and discounted conference registrations.
Student IEEE membership A discounted membership for students that includes access to research and career resources.
Software Life Cycle: Development phase The stage in which the software is developed.
Requirements analysis The process of determining the needs and constraints of stakeholders.
Design The process of creating a plan for software development.
Implementation The process of writing code for the software.
Testing The process of evaluating the software to identify and resolve errors and defects.
Glass box testing: Testing that takes into account the internal structure of the software.
Black box testing: Testing that evaluates the software without knowledge of its internal structure.
Alpha testing: Testing that takes place in-house before the software is released to the public.
Documentation: The written information that accompanies software.
User documentation: Documentation that is intended to help users understand and use the software.
System documentation: Documentation that describes the system's structure and components.
Technical documentation: Documentation that describes the software's technical details.
Intellectual property law: The legal protection of the rights of creators of original works, including software.
Software license: The legal agreement that outlines the terms and conditions for using software.
Software disclaimer: A statement that disclaims liability for any errors or defects in the software.
Best defenses against software liability claims: Adequate testing, clear documentation, and appropriate disclaimers.
